# 🧠 The Wembanyama Archetype Analysis

## 🎯 Goal

To determine whether Victor Wembanyama represents a unique player profile in the NBA.

---

## 📊 Methodology

We analyzed player data using three key metrics:

1. **Height** (in meters)
2. **3-point shooting percentage**
3. **Blocks per game**

We then created a composite score ("Wemby Score") using:

* Height (40%)
* Shooting (25%)
* Defense (35%)

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 1. Height alone is not enough

Many players are 7ft+, but most lack perimeter shooting or defensive versatility.

---

### 2. Shooting big men exist — but with limitations

Players like:

* Kristaps Porzingis
* Lauri Markkanen

show strong shooting, but differ in defensive impact.

---

### 3. Defensive anchors rarely shoot well

Traditional rim protectors:

* Hassan Whiteside
* Rudy Gobert

lack consistent perimeter shooting.

---

### 4. Closest modern comparisons

The closest players to Wembanyama include:

* Chet Holmgren
* Kristaps Porzingis

However, even these players do not fully match his combined profile.

---

## 🧠 Interpretation

Wembanyama is not just a "tall player who can shoot".

He represents a **convergence of traits** that are rarely seen together:

* Extreme height
* Perimeter shooting
* Elite rim protection

---

## 📈 Conclusion

Victor Wembanyama is part of a **new archetype of NBA player**.

As the game evolves, players with similar profiles may become more common — but currently, he remains an outlier.
